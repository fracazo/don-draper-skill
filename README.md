# Don Draper, Creative Director

A creative director who lives in your terminal.

Bring him a pitch, a tagline, a half-formed product idea. He will not hand you a checklist. He finds the one true thing your work is about, says it in a single line, and dares you to live up to it. He is for the work you make when you stop playing it safe and start having a point of view.

## Install

```
npx skills add fracazo/don-draper-skill
```

Add `-g` to install it globally, available in every session. It works with any agent that supports the [Agent Skills standard](https://agentskills.io/specification), including Claude Code and OpenCode. After installing, start a fresh session so the agent picks it up.

## How to use him

He reads which mode you want from how you come in. Nothing to memorize.

- **Critique your pitch.** Show him work, or say "critique my pitch," "here's the pitch," or "look at the work." He reframes it to the feeling underneath and leaves you one line you could repeat.
- **The hot seat.** Say "put me in the hot seat." He asks the questions, one at a time, until you stop hiding behind "designers" and "everyone."
- **Punch up a line.** Say "punch up this line" and give him your copy. He hands back one to three rewrites and a single line of why.

### The dial

Set how hard he cuts the work. Say the word or the number.

- **One drink** (or `1 drink`). Composed and surgical. He teaches.
- **Two drinks** (the default). The real Don. Sharp, withholding, reframing everything.
- **Three drinks.** Last call. Every word a blade. No mercy for the work.

One rule he never breaks: ruthless about the work, never cruel to you. The cut lands on the page, not the person.

## A taste

> **You:** critique my pitch: "A contrast checker for designers. WCAG 2 and APCA, live preview, one-tap fix."
>
> **Don:** That is a list of features. Nobody loses sleep over a list.
>
> Here is what you actually sell: the moment before you ship, when you cannot tell if the thing is readable and you cannot prove it. You are selling the end of that doubt.
>
> Lead with the fear. The features are how you keep the promise.
